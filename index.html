Introduction

Welcome to the Computational Design and Fabrication Research Group at the King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST). Our research focuses on two broad areas: (i)… , and (ii)….

Helmut Pottmann is a professor of Applied Mathematics and Computational Science Program (AMCS) at KAUST and Principal Investigator of CDF. He is also affiliated to the Visual Computing Center (VCC). His research interests are in Applied Geometry and Visual Computing, in particular in Geometric Modeling, Geometry Processing, and most recently, in Geometric Computing for Architecture and Manufacturing.

Openings

Postdoctoral Fellows

The CDF group at KAUST is looking for a top postdoctoral researcher with expertise in computer vision, machine learning, and/or optimization. The researcher for this opening will work at KAUST to investigate and develop principled computational methods for highly effective, scalable, and robust video modeling and analytics (e.g. action recognition, tracking, video registration, an object recognition in video), 3D understanding from RGBD, or deep learning (e.g. theory, methods, applications, and insight). Please send a full CV with a complete list of your publications and projects to Prof. Bernard Ghanem.

Students

The CDF group at KAUST is looking for motivated MS and Ph.D. students in computer vision and machine learning. Skills in (large-scale) optimization are a plus. Please send a full CV with a complete list of your publications and projects, as well as, an unofficial academic transcript to Prof. Bernard Ghanem.

Visiting Students

The CDF group at KAUST is looking for motivated visiting students or interns all throughout the year, including Fall, Spring, and Summer semesters. Skills in (large-scale) optimization are a plus. Please send a full CV with a complete list of your publications and projects, as well as, an unofficial academic transcript to Prof. Bernard Ghanem. Also, please consider applying to the Visiting Student Research Program (VSRP​) at KAUST.
